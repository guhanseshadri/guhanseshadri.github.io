About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Neurological Institute, Cleveland Clinic. My training is in biomedical engineering and computational signal analysis, with specialization in electrophysiology, brain network modeling, and machine learning.

I received my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from the National Institute of Technology Raipur. My doctoral research examined functional brain network organization in dyslexic children using EEG-based connectivity analysis, integrating graph-theoretic modeling with classification frameworks.

Prior to my current role, I served as Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at Amrita Vishva Vidyapeetham, Coimbatore, India, and as Ad-Hoc Faculty in Biomedical Engineering at NIT Raipur, where I taught biomedical signal processing, data science, and instrumentation while continuing research in electrophysiological network analysis.

Research Focus

I study large-scale brain network organization in epilepsy using MEG/EEG. My work identifies abnormal functional communities in interictal MEG recordings to understand how epileptogenic networks persist outside seizures.

Using source-space connectivity, graph theory, and advanced signal processing, I characterize pathological mesoscale topology and develop quantitative network biomarkers for epileptogenic zone prediction and surgical planning.
